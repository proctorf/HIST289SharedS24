Plain Dealer, Cleveland, Ohio, November 24, 1848
Link to XML

$900 REWARD will be paid for the apprehension and delivery of five runaway negroes to their owners in St. Louis, to wit: One hundred dollars for each one, and four hundred for the apprehension of the person who enticed them away, if he is convicted of the crime. The following is the description:

Priscilla, a girl belonging to Edward Bredell, rather tall and squares built, dark complexioned, about 22 or 23 years age, if confused would have impediment of speech.

Amanda, a girl belonging to Mrs. A. M. Perry, about 17 years old; dark complexioned; rather slout built, and of slow speech.

Nancy, a girl belonging to A. L. Allen; about 18 years old; stout, thick set, full face, flat nose, who is quick spoken; had on when she left, plaid gingham dress; wore a plain gold ring on her fore finger.

Theresa, a mulatto girl belonging to Barnard Pratte, aged about 17; well grown and very good looking; has a small wen in the corner of her right eye.

A negro man belonging to L. C. Dessaint, named Martin, about 33 years of age; spare built, about 5 feet 10 inches high, copper color, and his hair quite grey, quick spoken and a pretty smart boy.

EDWARD BREDELL.

A. L. ALLEN.

BERNARD PRATTE.

L. C. DESSAINT.
