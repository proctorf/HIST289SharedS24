20 dollars reward .

Ran away from the subscriber, on Saturday evening, the 9th inst. a negro man slave named Frank , aged about 35 or 36 years, five feet eight inches high, or thereabout. Said slave is very black, with white teeth, a pleasing countenance, very talkative to those he is acquainted with, but reserved to strangers; is fond of making use of high-sounding words; will deny stoutly being a runaway, but can be easily found out by being cross-questioned, where he is from, whom he knows, &c. When he went away he had on tow linen pantaloons, flax linen shirt, and probably took pantaloons of good quality, one dimity, and one blue cassimere vest.

A reward of $20 will be given for the apprehension and delivery of said slave to me in Lexington , if taken out of the state, or $10 if taken in the state, and all reasonable expenses paid, or the above mentioned reward paid if secured in any jail so that I can get him again.

Daniel Trotter .

Lexington, Ky. , October 12, 1829 . 710w3
