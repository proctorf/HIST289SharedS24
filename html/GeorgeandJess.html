Fifty Dollars Reward

Will be given for apprehending four negroes, viz.

A man by the name of George about 27 years of age, of a middle stature, and black complection, his wife is about 25 years old, of a yellow complection; they took with them two children, both boys, one four years old, the other fourteen months, the oldest boy's little finger and the next to it grows together on both hands. I will give the above reward to any person that will deliver them to me, living in Fayette County, on the head waters of Boons-Creek, and will pay all reasonable charges.

Likewise

Ranaway in the month of August 1796, a negro man, named Jess, he is now (if living) about 30 years of age, he is an active well made fellow, of a brown complexion, about five feet nine or ten inches high, and when standing bends in his knees, of a quick speech and if interrogated is apt to stammer. Any person who will deliver him to me, living on Boons-Creek Fayette County, shall receive one hundred dollars reward.

James Parish
